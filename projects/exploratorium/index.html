Here is an analysis of the scholar regarding the places where he was traveled. In the following map, you can see where this scholar traveled in the history. In this map, the bigger the radius of a circle is, the scholar visited that city more.

During the time this scholar is moved differently. Some year he moved more than another year. So, the following diagram shows the frequency of these travels. There are two y axis representing the absolute frequency and in the percentage. The x axis here shows the years regarding Islamic Lunar calender (Hijri).

Another interesting observation is the movements of this scholar between different cities. We build a graph in which the vertices represent the cities and the edges show the movement of this schlar between these cities. Since the graph can be relatively large, we filter the small frequencies. This threshold can be changed by the following slider,

1 20

Based on the selected threshold for the frequencies, the following graph is generated. The colors represent three different categories: onomastic data (names), toponymic onomastic data (toponymic names), and toponymic data (toponyms). The graph is drawn based on the force-directed visualization and it is moving until a equilibrium is achieved.

Network of entities

The graph can be similarly visualized using a heatmap. So, the axes are the cities and the value of the heatmap shows the frequency of travel between these cities. The values are shown by colors here. When a color is stronger (nearer to black), the frequency is higher.
